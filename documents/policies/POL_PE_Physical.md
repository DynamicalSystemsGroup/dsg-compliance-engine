# Physical Access Policy (Office and Remote Work Sites)

**Organization:** Dynamical Systems Group, LLC
**Document ID:** POL-PE-001
**Version:** 1.0
**Last Reviewed:** 2026-06-15
**Next Review:** 2027-06-15
**Applies To:** All DSG personnel, contractors, and visitors at any DSG-designated work site.

## Purpose

Dynamical Systems Group (DSG) is a fully remote small business (~10 personnel). This policy
covers physical protection of CUI at DSG's registered office of record and at each
employee's alternate (home) work site. It implements NIST SP 800-171 Rev.2 controls:

- **PE.L2-3.10.3** — Escort visitors and monitor visitor activity.
- **PE.L2-3.10.4** — Maintain audit logs of physical access.
- **PE.L2-3.10.5** — Control and manage physical access devices.
- **PE.L2-3.10.6** — Enforce safeguarding measures for CUI at alternate work sites.

## PE.L2-3.10.3 — Visitor Escort

Any visitor entering a DSG-controlled space (office of record or an employee's home office
during a work-related visit) must be escorted by a DSG employee for the duration of the
visit. Visitors may not be left alone in rooms where CUI is displayed, printed, or stored.

## PE.L2-3.10.4 — Visitor Log

Visitors sign the visitor log maintained at
`documents/evidence/physical/visitor-log.md`. Each entry captures name, affiliation, purpose,
DSG escort, arrival time, and departure time. The Operations lead reviews the log monthly.

## PE.L2-3.10.5 — Physical Access Device Management

Physical access devices (office badges, keys, alarm codes) are issued to individuals by name.
Issuance and revocation are recorded in `documents/evidence/physical/access-devices.md`.
Devices are revoked or reclaimed on role change, offboarding, or loss. The Operations lead
reviews the roster quarterly and reconciles against the current employee list.

## PE.L2-3.10.6 — Alternate Work Site (Home Office) Safeguards

Because DSG is fully remote, each employee's home office is treated as an alternate work
site. Each employee signs a **Remote Work Agreement** (`documents/agreements/remote-work/`)
that requires:

1. A lockable room, drawer, or cabinet for any printed CUI.
2. Screen positioning to prevent shoulder-surfing by household members or visitors.
3. Prohibition on discussing CUI in shared or public spaces.
4. Immediate reporting of theft, loss, or unauthorized access.
5. Return or destruction of any physical CUI at offboarding.
